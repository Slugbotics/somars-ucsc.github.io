About us

Building the full
autonomous stack.

SOMARS — Student Organization for Marine and Aerial Robotics Systems — is UC Santa Cruz's competitive drone team under Slugbotics. We design, fabricate, and fly UAVs capable of fully autonomous missions, competing against top engineering programs nationwide.

Our members span computer science, electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, and more — collaborating across every layer of the stack, from carbon fiber airframes to computer-vision-guided navigation.

Competitions

Where we compete.

SOMARS enters two of the most rigorous student UAV competitions in the country, requiring fully autonomous systems capable of real-world mission performance.

01

C-UASC

Collegiate Unmanned Aircraft Systems Competition

Tests student teams on autonomous fixed-wing and multi-rotor operations — waypoint navigation, payload drop, and autonomous landing. Scored on mission completion, technical reports, and oral presentations.

Waypoint Nav Payload Drop Auto Landing

02

SUAS

Student Unmanned Aerial Systems Competition · AUVSI

One of the most prestigious student UAV competitions. Teams complete an autonomous search-and-rescue-style mission: obstacle avoidance, target identification, and payload delivery.

Obstacle Avoidance Target ID Payload Delivery

Our approach

Simulation-first.
Hardware-proven.

Every algorithm is validated in Gazebo before it ever touches real hardware — reducing crash risk and dramatically accelerating iteration speed.

Autonomous Navigation

Waypoint planning and path execution with no human input in the loop.

Computer Vision

Real-time target detection and obstacle avoidance onboard the aircraft.

Simulation Pipeline

Full Gazebo-based testing environment before every real-world flight.

Custom Embedded Systems

In-house firmware and tight flight controller integration.
