Structure

Why subteams?

Autonomous drones span mechanical engineering, electronics, real-time software, computer vision, and simulation. Subteams let members specialize deeply while collaborating toward a single flying vehicle.

Each subteam owns a core layer of the drone stack, working in parallel so progress moves fast across the entire system.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do I have to pick just one subteam?

Not necessarily. Most members focus on one subteam first to build depth, then branch out once they're comfortable.

Can I switch subteams?

Yes — talk with the subteam leads and we'll help you transition. People switch when their interests evolve or they want a new challenge.

How experienced do I need to be?

No prior drone or robotics experience required. Curiosity and consistency matter far more than what you already know.

What majors are welcome?

All majors. We need mechanical engineers, computer scientists, electrical engineers, and anyone excited about autonomous systems.

Join Us

How to get involved.

01

Complete SlugWorks Canvas & join Discord

Most of our work happens in SlugWorks (BE-49). Complete the safety training Canvas module and join our Discord to stay in the loop on meetings and announcements.

Discord and Canvas links coming soon
02

Visit a few subteam meetings

Check meeting times on each subteam page. If times don't work, reach out — we reschedule at the start of every quarter to fit everyone.

03

Commit to a subteam

Once you've explored, pick a primary subteam and start taking on tasks — CAD, firmware, vision code, simulation scripts, whatever the group needs.
