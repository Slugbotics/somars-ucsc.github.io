About Us

MATE is UC Santa Cruz's competitive marine robotics team. We design, build, and test underwater remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) for the annual MATE ROV World Championship.

We provide students of all different majors with hands on engineering experiences in software, electrical and mechanical.

What is the MATE ROV Competition?

The MATE ROV Competition is an international underwater robotics challenge built around real marine engineering problems. University teams construct ROVs capable of operating underwater to complete mission tasks that simulate real field operations.

2025 Season

The 2025 season marked our first time competiting at the MATE ROV competition after the pandemic. This event marked a major milestone for the team and allowed us to build off our knowledge for the future.

Our ROV was designed ot tackle tasks like analysing shipwrecks (Determining their class and size). Replacing broken fuses with our arm. Takine water samples and testing the PH levels.

We hope to compete at Canada for 2026!
