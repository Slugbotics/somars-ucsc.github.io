Simulations

Using Gazebo to visualize, validate, and simulate autonomous flight before real-world testing.

Who We Are

The Simulations subteam builds the virtual world where our autonomous drone learns to fly. Using Gazebo, we create realistic 3D environments that replicate competition arenas, terrain, and obstacles — allowing the entire autonomy stack to be tested safely before any real flight.

Our work dramatically reduces the risk of crashes and dramatically speeds up iteration: bugs in the control stack can be caught and fixed in simulation at near-zero cost.

What We Work On

🏟️ Competition World Models

Building accurate Gazebo models of the SUAS and C-UASC competition arenas — including waypoint fields, obstacle configurations, and target placement zones — so the team can rehearse full mission runs.

✈️ Drone Model & Physics

Creating a high-fidelity URDF / SDF model of our physical drone with accurate mass, inertia, motor thrust curves, and aerodynamic drag so simulated behavior matches real flight.

🔁 Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)

Setting up Software-in-the-Loop (SITL) and Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) pipelines with ArduPilot / PX4 so the flight controller firmware runs against the simulated plant in real time.

📐 Flight Path Validation

Simulation Environment

Our primary simulation stack:

  • Simulator: Gazebo (Harmonic / Classic)
  • Middleware: ROS 2
  • Flight stack integration: ArduPilot SITL / PX4 SITL via MAVROS
  • Visualization: RViz 2, Gazebo GUI
  • Drone model format: SDF / URDF
  • Operating system: Ubuntu 22.04
